## Why It Gets Harder to Build Muscle After 40

Here's the part most fitness advice skips.

The problem isn't willpower. It's a switch in your body called mTOR — short for "mechanistic target of rapamycin," the enzyme that signals your muscles to grow.

After about age 40, mTOR gets sluggish. Your body stops responding to protein the way it did at 25. You can eat the chicken breast, drink the shake, and your muscles still don't get the memo.

That's why guys who used to bench 225 in college suddenly find themselves working harder for smaller gains. The raw materials are there. The signal isn't.

This is also why eating more protein past 40 has diminishing returns. You can't fix a signaling problem by dumping more raw material into a system that isn't listening. You need something that wakes the listener up.

Dileucine is a dipeptide — two leucine molecules bonded together. According to peer-reviewed research published in *Frontiers in Physiology* (2023) and a PLOS ONE trial, dileucine triggers a stronger muscle protein synthesis signal than standard free leucine.

The number you'll see cited most often: dileucine boosts muscle protein synthesis by 159% compared to baseline. Why does bonding two leucines together beat single leucine? The dipeptide form survives digestion better and reaches muscle tissue more intact, where it delivers a stronger mTOR signal per gram. You're getting more signaling punch from less total material.

### myHMB® (1.5 grams)

HMB is the metabolite your body produces from leucine. Its job is the flip side of dileucine: instead of turning growth on, it slows muscle breakdown.

The figure that matters: research cited on the official product page shows MyHMB reduces muscle loss by 57%. In one study referenced by the manufacturer, even bedridden adults retained significantly more muscle mass with HMB supplementation than those without it.

So one ingredient flips the switch on. The other stops the leak. Most supplements address only one half of the equation.
